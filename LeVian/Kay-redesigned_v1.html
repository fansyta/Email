  WHAT’S INSPIRING:
A Le Vian Historical Flashback
  

 
 

A Spectacular Objet d’art
from Le Vian

Our inspiration is fueled by our historic past, from our earliest ancestors who guarded the priceless royal jewels of King Nadir Shah centuries ago, to our beloved father, Abdulrahim LeVian, who immigrated to the U.S. and established the company in New York, creating the most spectacular designs featuring handset invisible settings. The year 2000 marked a milestone in our historical journey, the realization of our transformation into THE trendsetting Fashion House of Fine Jewelry.

Here, in “What’s Inspiring?,” we will introduce to you a piece from Le Vian Through the Centuries, our family’s archive of jewelry and objets d’art dating back to the 1500s. These are the bejeweled threads that weave together our past, present and future.

 
This jeweled elephant, circa 1746, stands 11 inches tall, is 12 inches long and weighs 40 pounds! It features 6,602 pieces (12,000 carats!) of natural turquoise, all hand inlaid, from the legendary Neishabur mine in Persia, which is now extinct. Each eye is a 2-carat round ruby, and the tusks are hand-carved ivory. The quality of the workmanship is testament to the extraordinary artistry and expertise of the LeVian family.
